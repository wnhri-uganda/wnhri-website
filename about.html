About the organization

West Nile Hope & Resilience Initiative

Faith-based care for dignity, hope, and resilience in West Nile.

Our background

Born from the realities of life in West Nile.

West Nile Hope & Resilience Initiative was founded in response to the growing needs of orphans, young people, women, persons with disabilities, older persons, and families affected by addiction, poverty, chronic illness, social exclusion, and climate change.

As population growth and refugee settlement place greater pressure on community resources, WNHRI brings care closer to home. Our faith-based, community-led approach combines healing, practical support, empowerment, and sustainable livelihoods so vulnerable people can recover their dignity and build lasting resilience.

Our aim To provide holistic rehabilitation, care, and empowerment services to vulnerable communities in West Nile and beyond.
Our purpose

Hope that heals. Support that lasts.

Our mission and vision guide every community visit, partnership, and program.

01 Vision

A West Nile where every vulnerable life is healed, empowered, and sustained with dignity and resilience.

02 Mission

To restore dignity and strengthen resilience among vulnerable communities through rehabilitation, care, empowerment, climate adaptation, and organizational support.

How we work

Community-based care, from immediate need to lasting resilience.

01

Listen locally

We work through families, parishes, community networks, schools, and local leaders to understand needs and identify people at risk.

02

Care holistically

We connect emotional, spiritual, social, educational, health, and livelihood support instead of treating each challenge in isolation.

03

Build capability

Training, counseling, savings groups, skills development, and organizational support help people and local institutions become more self-reliant.

04

Partner for impact

We collaborate with health facilities, faith institutions, schools, government, civil society, and development partners to extend our reach.

Our home

Rooted in West Nile, Uganda.

Our central office is at Odianyadri Trading Centre, Eruba Parish, Vurra Sub County, Arua District—about five kilometres from Arua City along the Arua–Nebbi–Pakwach road.
